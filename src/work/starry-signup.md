**At a Glance**
- **Timeline:** 3 phases over 12 months (initial phase: 7 weeks)
- **Scope:** Mobile and desktop signup redesign with modular architecture for future expansion
- **Role:** Senior UX consultant leading strategy, research, and design across product, creative, marketing, and engineering teams
- **Key challenge:** No in-house UX, tight deadlines, need to add complexity without hurting conversion

---

## Outcome

**What I delivered:**

A complete mobile and desktop signup redesign with a modular architecture that could accommodate future business models—plan customization and paid add-ons—without compromising brand clarity or conversion.

**Core deliverables:**
- **Information architecture** that allowed engineering to start building while UI was still in development
- **Usability testing framework** with 10+ participants across 3 rounds to validate direction before executive review
- **Wireframes and prototypes** for mobile and desktop signup flows
- **Brand positioning framework** defining how to introduce paid features transparently (adopted across marketing and product)
- **Implementation documentation** and A/B testing plan for post-launch optimization

**Impact:**

The work launched on schedule and passed C-suite review with minimal revisions (rare for this team). Post-launch monitoring showed the redesigned flow maintained strong conversion performance and customer satisfaction scores despite adding considerable complexity—a result the team had been concerned about going in. 

The foundation supported two additional project phases over the following year. The testing approach I designed became the company's standard for product launches.

---

## Context

A growing internet provider needed to evolve its customer signup experience to support new product offerings and business models while preserving the brand clarity and ease of use that had earned it one of the strongest customer satisfaction scores in the industry.

---

## Challenge

**The core problem:** The existing signup flow worked well but wasn't built to scale. Any changes needed to accommodate increased complexity—new plans, paid add-ons, hardware options—without adding friction, confusing customers, or compromising conversion. 

**The constraints:** The company had no in-house UX function. The initial project had a **7-week deadline** to redesign the core experience and establish a foundation for future expansion. That foundation then supported **two additional phases** over the following year: exploratory research on onboarding and customization, followed by a framework for introducing paid add-ons in a way that felt brand-aligned rather than exploitative.

---

## My Role

I worked as a senior UX consultant embedded with product, creative, marketing, and engineering teams across **three phases spanning roughly a year**. I led **UX strategy and research**, and worked closely with the creative director and lead designer on overall design direction. 

I also designed the project structure itself to keep cross-functional teams aligned and moving in parallel under tight deadlines. A core part of my role was bringing stakeholders in at the right moments to maintain momentum without sacrificing buy-in.

---

## Approach

**Information architecture first.** Starting with IA and research frameworks before jumping into design let teams work in parallel and make decisions with confidence instead of waiting on each other.

**Research-led iteration.** Multiple rounds of usability testing with real users shaped the work throughout the process, not just at the end. We iterated based on evidence, not assumptions.

**Strategic stakeholder timing.** Bringing executives in at the right moments—early enough to get their input, late enough that we had something concrete to show—kept us moving without getting stuck in endless review cycles.

**Reusable systems.** Testing frameworks, brand guidelines, and modular design patterns that could support future work without needing to start from scratch.

---

## How I Contributed

**Designed a research-led workflow** that let engineering begin implementation while UI was still in development, compressing the timeline without sacrificing quality.

**Built a brand positioning framework** that defined how to introduce paid features transparently—later adopted across marketing and product strategy.

**Managed stakeholder involvement strategically** to secure buy-in with minimal revisions (described by the team as unusually smooth for executive review).

**Led discovery work** that surfaced overlooked opportunities and informed pricing strategy, messaging, and product roadmap beyond my direct scope.

**Established a usability testing approach** that became the company's standard process for product launches.

---

## What Followed

Building on the initial foundation, I continued working with the team through two more phases: exploratory research on onboarding and customization, followed by designing a framework for introducing paid add-ons. The work provided a foundation that enabled future product evolution while preserving the brand's emphasis on transparency and ease of use.
